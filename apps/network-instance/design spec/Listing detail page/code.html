Architectural Philosophy

Designed by the visionary Kristján Sigurd, The Obsidian Monolith represents a radical departure from traditional Icelandic residential forms. The structure is carved into the volcanic landscape, utilizing raw basalt and charred cedar to create a seamless dialogue between the internal sanctuary and the rugged exterior terrain.

Floor-to-ceiling triple-glazed apertures frame the ever-shifting Northern Lights, while the interior layout follows a rhythmic sequence of double-height volumes and intimate decompression zones. This is not merely a residence; it is a curated sensory experience designed for those who seek silence and visual clarity.
